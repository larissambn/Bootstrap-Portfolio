Ponta Grossa Tourism

The city

Ponta Grossa is located in the Second Plateau of Paraná, in the Campos Gerais region. It stands out in the tourist and economic scenario of southern Brazil, due to its geographical position and easy access to all regions of the State. An important road-rail junction, the city has its roots in tropeirismo, ethnic plurality and railway paths, historical symbols and landmarks still present in the urban setting of one of the most important Brazilian cities. Part of the Rota dos Tropeiros, Ponta Grossa brings together a complex of natural, historical and cultural attractions that unfold amidst the undulating landscape of Campos Gerais and provide its visitors with multiple opportunities for leisure, culture and tourism. The contrast between the ancient and the modern, the immensity and beauty of its nature and cultural manifestations are decisive factors in the city's recognition as a tourist and cultural hub in Brazil. In addition to the quality of its hotel and gastronomic network, Ponta Grossa also offers a pleasant night out with varied aspects that guarantee fun for all tastes.

The settlement and development

The first news of occupation of our region was in 1704. Afterwards, part of the land was donated to the Jesuit priests who built the Santa Bárbara Chapel on the site (Pitangui). There, several farms appeared. During their travels, farmers stopped to rest and eat in places that came to be called ranches or “landings”. From these landings, towns emerged, such as Castro and Ponta Grossa. Ponta Grossa was elevated to Parish status on September 15, 1823 and a location was chosen on top of a hill for the construction of a new chapel in honor of Senhora Sant’Ana. This location was chosen to be the headquarters of the Parish and housing and commercial houses began to be built around it. This hill is where the Cathedral of Sant’Ana stands today. In 1855, Ponta Grossa was elevated to a town and in 1862 to a city. More and more people arrived here, and the city grew and developed, becoming the most important in the interior of Paraná. It was with the arrival of the Railway tracks that Ponta Grossa became a large commercial, cultural and social center. The railway transformed the city into a major junction, standing out in the Campos Gerais Region and Paraná. This made countless people choose the place to work, study and live. It was at this time that immigrants arrived, contributing to the city's ever-increasing growth. Ukrainians, Germans, Poles, Italians, Russians, Syrians and Lebanese, among many others, settled here, contributing to the growth of the city, as well as the social, political, economic and cultural development of Ponta Grossa. Ponta Grossa stood out in the 20th century, with many commercial stores, industries, schools, cinemas, theaters, newspapers, libraries, among others. It can be said that that small village, which emerged as a landing place for drovers, grew and became a large city.
